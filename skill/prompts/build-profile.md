# Prompt: build-profile

Fill the user's `profile.md` and `accomplishments.md` from their resume. This is
the same prompt `jl profile build` emits on stdout, with the resume text appended.

## What to do

You are helping build a job-seeker profile. Read the RESUME provided, then
create or fill these files at the data-dir root:

- `profile.md`: who you are (summary, strengths, target role "lanes", domains,
  seniority) AND what you want next (target titles/lanes, target seniority, salary floor/target,
  remote/location and relocation, company stage/size, tech, mission/industry,
  timeline/urgency, hard dealbreakers).
- `accomplishments.md`: the master accomplishment prose plus remixable STAR
  beats, pulled from the resume and expanded with any detail the user gives you.

The scaffolded files already contain section headings and guidance comments.
Fill them in; keep the structure.

## Rules

- **Ask up to 5 clarifying questions first** if anything is ambiguous
  (especially salary floor, location/relocation, and dealbreakers, which a
  resume rarely states). Wait for answers before writing those sections.
- Keep it **concise and factual**. No filler, no invented experience.
- Pull strengths and accomplishments from what the resume actually shows.
- Where the resume is silent on a preference, leave a clear `TODO:` marker
  rather than guessing.

## Source

Read the resume from `resume/resume.txt` in the data dir (generated by
`jl resume set`). If you were piped the resume via `jl profile build`, it
is appended below under `RESUME:`.
